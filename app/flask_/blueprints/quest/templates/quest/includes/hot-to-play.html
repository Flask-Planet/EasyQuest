Health, Attacking, and Defending:

Health is usually balanced around 100, with weapons causing around 10 per successful hit.

The player's stats and the current weapon will contribute to the amount of damage caused on a successful hit.

Defence works on matching the dice roll of the attacker. The attacker must roll higher than the defence stat.

Defence cannot be 0, and it cannot be 6.

Here's defence scenarios:

If the player's defence is 3; The enemy will need to roll a 4 or above to land a successful hit. A roll of 3 matches the players defence, and is therefore blocked.
If the player's defence is 1; The enemy will need to roll a 2 or above to land a successful hit. If the enemy rolls a 1, this is treated as a miss.
If the player's defence is 5; The enemy will need to roll a 6 to land a successful hit. The player cannot be invulnerable, rolling a 6 ensures this.

Stats & How They Work:

Each stat has a different purpose and will be used in different scenarios. The checks will be done by the Quest Master, using common sense on when to check.

  • Strength: Used for physical strength checks.
  • Agility: Used for quick actions and athletic ability checks.
  • Intelligence: Used for identification and knowledge checks.
  • Luck: Used for random events and chance checks.
  • Perception: Used for awareness, and spotting hidden items, enemies and traps.
  • Persuasion: Used for bartering, gas lighting, and or talking your way out of (or in to) situations.

All stat rolls are based on a 6-sided die.

The player's stat works by subtracting the value of the stat from the player's roll.

Let's take the player's Strength as an example, here's a scenario:

If the player's Strength is 4 ( 6 − 4 = 2 ); The player will need to roll above a 2 to be successful. In this case the player will need to roll a 3 or above.
If the player's Strength is 1 ( 6 − 1 = 5 ); The player will need to roll above a 5 to be successful. In this case the player will need to roll a 6.
A stat with 0 will always fail.

The Rules of Effects:

Effects are temporary changes to the player's stats and will be used in different scenarios.

  • Poisoned: 10 damage each turn, this will last until the encounter is over.
  • Sleeping: Unable to do any actions. This will last until you are hit.
  • Confused: You run the risk of attacking teammates, or wondering into bad places. This will last two turns.
  • Buffed: Your damage output is increased. This will last until the encounter is over.
